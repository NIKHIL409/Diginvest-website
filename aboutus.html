Description:

Finance and investment is a general term for the conduit between those individuals and companies that have extra money (capital) and others that have the need and use for that capital.

It can take many forms from debt instruments such as loans, bonds, and mortgages to equity such as stocks and partnerships. In addition banks and credit unions are all financial institutions. The size of the finance and investment market is huge. There are 6 billion shares of stock traded each day just on the NYSE alone and the bond market may be 10x larger than the stock market.

The investment markets are very efficient at connecting these people together for the mutual benefits for both parties.

Stocks, Bonds, most businesses and corporations, real estate, automobiles, credit cards, etc are examples of industries that operate on “other peoples money”. In normal times over 90% of the real estate sold included some type of financing.
